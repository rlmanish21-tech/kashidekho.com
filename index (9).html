Sacred Places in Kashi

The Soul of Every Itinerary

01

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas — the most sacred temple of Shiva. Gold-plated shikhara, rebuilt in 1780 by Maharani Ahilya Bai Holkar. Every pilgrimage begins here.

Explore ›
02

Dashashwamedh Ghat Aarti

Every evening, priests perform the Ganga Aarti with fire, flowers and chanting. A spectacle of devotion that transforms the river into living light.

Explore ›
03

The 84 Ghats of Kashi

84 stone steps descend to the Ganga — each ghat with its own deity, story and ritual. A pre-dawn boat ride is among the most profound experiences in India.

Explore ›
04

Manikarnika Ghat

The eternal cremation ghat of Kashi — where a sacred flame has burned for over 3,500 years. Hindus believe dying here grants moksha, liberation from the cycle of rebirth.

Explore ›
05

Sarnath

Where the Buddha delivered his first sermon after enlightenment. The Dhamek Stupa, Ashoka Pillar and Sarnath Museum hold some of the most significant Buddhist artifacts in the world.

Explore ›
06

The Old City & Banarasi Galis

Narrow lanes winding past silk weavers, paan shops, sweet sellers and ancient havelis. The old city is a living museum where 3,000 years of unbroken culture continues today.
